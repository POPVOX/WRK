## The Scenario: Tennessee River Valley Flooding

**Date:** March 2027

**Event:** Prolonged rainfall causes catastrophic flooding across 12 counties in Middle and West Tennessee. The Tennessee and Cumberland Rivers overflow simultaneously. 47 lives lost. 23,000 homes damaged or destroyed. $4.2 billion in estimated damages. Three interstates closed. Water treatment plants offline in four cities.

**Congressional Delegation:**
- 9 House Members (4 with affected districts)
- 2 Senators
- Relevant committees: Homeland Security, Transportation & Infrastructure, Appropriations

**Agencies Involved:**
- FEMA (lead federal agency)
- Army Corps of Engineers
- HUD (housing assistance)
- SBA (disaster loans)
- USDA (agricultural losses)
- DOT (infrastructure)
- HHS (public health)
- Tennessee Emergency Management Agency (state)

### What Makes This Possible

1. **Decision Trace Layer**: Every interaction, decision, and commitment is captured and queryable. Without this, agents have no memory.

2. **Virtual Collaboration Spaces**: Agents can coordinate across organizational boundaries while respecting data sovereignty. Without this, it's just 11 separate AI assistants.

3. **Real-Time Information Flows**: Ground truth is continuously aggregated from multiple sources. Without this, agents operate on stale data.

4. **Human-in-the-Loop Architecture**: Agents propose, humans approve. Trust is maintained because human judgment remains central. Without this, no office would participate.

5. **Shared Protocols**: All participants agree on data formats, escalation paths, and coordination rules. Without this, agents can't interoperate.
